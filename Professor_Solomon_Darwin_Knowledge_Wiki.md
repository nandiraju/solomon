# Professor Solomon Darwin — Knowledge Wiki

## 1. Entity Summary

**Solomon N. Darwin** is an American professor of business, author, innovation practitioner, speaker, and academic leader. He is best known for his work in **open innovation**, **business-model innovation**, **emerging markets**, and the **Smart Village Movement**.

Darwin currently serves as **Director of Open Innovation at the University of California, Santa Cruz Silicon Valley Campus**. He previously spent more than two decades in leadership and teaching roles at the **University of California, Berkeley Haas School of Business**, most prominently as Executive Director of the **Garwood Center for Corporate Innovation**. He is also described as Director Emeritus of the Berkeley-Haas corporate innovation center.

His work combines corporate innovation, academic research, executive education, public-private partnerships, digital inclusion, and rural development. His signature contribution is the development of smart-village frameworks designed to connect rural communities with digital infrastructure, education, healthcare, financial services, entrepreneurship, and global markets.

## 3. Canonical Biography

Solomon Darwin began his professional career in corporate finance, banking, and technology before moving into university teaching and academic leadership. His corporate experience included work at Motorola, Bank of America, First Interstate Bank, and financial-services organizations.

From 1996 to 2005, he served as an Associate Professor at the University of Southern California. In 2005, he joined the Haas School of Business at the University of California, Berkeley. At Berkeley, he led centers focused on financial reporting, financial technology, and corporate innovation.

Darwin later became Executive Director of the Garwood Center for Corporate Innovation, where he worked closely with Professor Henry Chesbrough, a leading scholar associated with the concept of open innovation. Darwin helped translate open-innovation theory into corporate programs, business-model frameworks, executive forums, teaching cases, and emerging-market initiatives.

His work eventually expanded beyond corporate innovation to rural economic development. Through the Smart Village Movement, he developed frameworks for using digital technology and open-innovation ecosystems to improve opportunities in underserved villages. Mori, a village in Andhra Pradesh, India, became an early prototype for this approach.

Darwin has authored or co-authored books on smart villages, sustainability, caste and social mobility, and India's development. He continues to teach, speak, advise, and promote open innovation and smart-village development.

## 9. Smart Village Movement

### 9.1 Overview

The **Smart Village Movement** is an innovation and rural-development initiative associated with Darwin's work at UC Berkeley Haas.

Its purpose is to improve rural communities through digital technology, open innovation, entrepreneurship, and collaboration among universities, companies, governments, nonprofits, and residents.

### 9.2 Definition

Darwin describes a smart village as:

> A community empowered by digital technologies and open-innovation platforms to access global markets.

### 9.3 Guiding Principles

- Villages should be participants in innovation, not passive recipients of aid.
- Digital access should lead to economic and social opportunity.
- Local needs should shape technology and business-model design.
- Government, business, academia, and communities should work together.
- Solutions should be scalable and economically sustainable.
- Rural residents should gain access to information, services, tools, and markets.

### 9.4 Major Components

| Component | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Connectivity | Reliable internet and digital access |
| Education | Digital literacy, training, and remote learning |
| Healthcare | Telemedicine and improved access to care |
| Agriculture | Market information, technology, and productivity tools |
| Financial inclusion | Banking, payments, credit, and insurance access |
| Entrepreneurship | Local businesses, jobs, and innovation |
| E-governance | Easier access to public services and information |
| Market access | Connections to regional and global buyers |
| Partnerships | Coordination across public, private, academic, and nonprofit sectors |

### 9.5 Mori Village Prototype

**Mori**, a village in Andhra Pradesh, India, became the best-known early prototype of Darwin's smart-village framework.

The Mori initiative explored how digital infrastructure and ecosystem partnerships could improve:

- Internet access.
- Education.
- Healthcare.
- Banking.
- Agriculture.
- Entrepreneurship.
- Women's economic participation.
- Access to government and commercial services.

The Mori work became the basis for books, white papers, research, teaching cases, and broader smart-village initiatives.

### 9.6 Geographic Expansion

Smart-village concepts associated with Darwin have been discussed or applied in multiple regions of India, including:

- Andhra Pradesh.
- Arunachal Pradesh.
- Other rural and emerging-market settings.

### 9.7 Why the Model Is Distinctive

The model combines two objectives:

1. Improving quality of life and access to essential services.
2. Creating sustainable business and entrepreneurship opportunities.

This distinguishes the smart-village approach from purely charitable or infrastructure-only rural-development models.

## 22. Compact Agent Context

Solomon N. Darwin is an American professor, author, and open-innovation practitioner. He serves as Director of Open Innovation at the University of California, Santa Cruz Silicon Valley Campus and previously led the Garwood Center for Corporate Innovation at UC Berkeley Haas. He is known for open innovation, business-model innovation, emerging-market strategy, and the Smart Village Movement. His smart-village model uses digital connectivity and partnerships among governments, universities, companies, nonprofits, entrepreneurs, and communities to improve rural access to education, healthcare, finance, markets, and employment. Mori in Andhra Pradesh, India, became an early prototype. Darwin previously taught at the University of Southern California and worked in finance, banking, and technology. His books include *The Untouchables*, *Smart Villages of Tomorrow: The Road to Mori*, *How to Think Like the CEO of the Planet*, *How to Create Smart Villages*, and *Resetting the Jewel in the Crown*.
